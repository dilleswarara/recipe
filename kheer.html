kheer

Kheer is a traditional Indian dessert made from rice, milk, and sugar. Here's a step-by-step process to make delicious kheer:

ingrediance:

  • * liter of full-fat milk
  • * 1/4 cup basmati rice (or any other fragrant rice)
  • * 1/4 to 1/2 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • * 2-3 green cardamom pods (crushed)
  • * A pinch of saffron strands (optional)
  • * 2 tablespoons chopped nuts (almonds, cashews, pistachios)
  • * 1-2 tablespoons raisins (optional)
  • * 1 teaspoon ghee (clarified butter)

process:

To make kheer, start by washing and soaking 1/4 cup of rice for about 30 minutes. In a heavy-bottomed pan, bring 1 liter of milk to a boil, then reduce the heat to a simmer. Drain the soaked rice and add it to the simmering milk. Cook the mixture on low heat, stirring occasionally to prevent the rice from sticking to the bottom. Continue to cook until the rice is fully cooked and the milk has thickened, which usually takes about 45 minutes. Add 1/4 cup of sugar and stir well until it dissolves completely. Flavor the kheer with a pinch of cardamom powder and a few strands of saffron soaked in a tablespoon of warm milk. Let the kheer simmer for another 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together. Finally, garnish with chopped nuts such as almonds, pistachios, and cashews. Serve the kheer warm or chilled, depending on your preference.
